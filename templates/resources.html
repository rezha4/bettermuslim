"Asyhadu an laa ilaha illallah, wa asyhadu anna muhammadar rasulullah."
It reads: "I bear witness that there is no deity but God, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of God." this states that you truly submits your will into allah’s & muhammad SAW will, so anything allah forbids, you also forbids yourself from it, and anything allah will you to do, you do it. Do it in moderation and do it as best as possible
Submitting your will to God and His last messenger is an act of putting your trust in their judgements (God’s and Muhammad’s). Because really, you have no way of knowing what lies ahead of time, sometimes you don’t even know if something is good or bad

There are 5 daily prayers, its time is determined by various positions of the sun.

  1. Subuh: 2 units of prayer. When the sun is about to rise.
  2. Dzuhur: 4 units of prayer. When the sun is just about to move to the west, after reaching its zenith.
  3. Ashr: 4 units of prayer. When the sun is a third way into setting.
  4. Maghrib: 3 units of prayer. When the sun is about to set.
  5. Isya: 4 units of prayer. When the sun is completely set and no sunlight present.
Further readings: The philosophy of 5 daily prayers
5 daily prayers at specific time is tied to the remembrance of Allah. It is also an act of always purifying yourself, physically with wudhu, and mentally, throughout the day.

On the holy month of Ramdahan, Allah requests of you to do fasting, from sunrise to sunset. There is a significant health benefit in doing fasting.
But truly, doing this is above health reasons, it is an act of taqwa, act of submission to God.

Every end of Ramadan, there is zakat fitrah. And every 1 hijri year, if you had a disposable income the worth of 85 grams of gold, you are obligated to donate 2,5%. Read more: Zakat
This act of charity tied tightly with the act of Islam that does not only focus on your relationship with Allah, but also your relationship with others.

The pilgrimage to Mecca is obligatory once you have the money and energy to do so. Read more: Hajj.
This is a manifestation of the mental and physical submission to Allah. You will go through a series of acts of worship, within 5 to 6 days of Hajj.
